Mobile Rewards Application

Infosys Client



One of the difficulties with the existing application was that users were not clearly notified of an active time frame for each rewards' use. We proposed timestamps/markers on the home screen to let users know how much time they had to take advantage of a reward.






Customers often expressed dissatisfaction with the rewards available to them. We thought that a filter that could display relevant rewards based on category could help users find rewards that they would enjoy.

Additionally, many users reported seeing rewards advertised that were not geographically accessible for them. We proposed adding location services to the application, so rewards could display depending on which offers were physically accessible.

The Challenge

Our client is a leading mobile service provider. They have a mobile application for delivering rewards to customers, such as free/discounted food, travel deals, gas rewards, and limited merchandise. Our goal was to make their rewards application easier to use and more satisfying for customers. In other words, we wanted customers to feel like having this application, and redeeming rewards regularly, was "worth it."

The Goal

We wanted to increase the usage of this rewards application by minimizing the pains associated with its use - uninteresting rewards, multiple logins, and external registrations. We wanted to present rewards in such a way that users would find them easy to access, redeem, and enjoy.

Approach

We conducted an extensive research process that included surveying, in-person interviewing, heuristic evaluaution, and journey mapping. We distributed an online survey to as many potential users of this mobile provider that we could access. We also visited several brick-and-mortar stores for the service provider, and spoke with visitors to the store about their experiences with the application.

After compiling and analyzing our findings, we transitioned into in-office analysis of the original application features. We used Nielsen Norman Group's Usability Heuristics to assess the usability of the current application, and addressed any shortcomings in the redesign.

The Outcome

Our team presented the proposed redesign to the client. The client currently uses Infosys's services for back end software development.






One of the key issues with the original rewards application was that, when redeeming a reward, users often were redirected outside of the app, either to a partnering company's app or to the browser. This could be discouraging for users, who did not want to put their credit card and/or email address into a different company's application. We felt that the most streamlined way for users to redeem rewards would be an in-app QR code that populated when the user tapped a "Redeem" button.
