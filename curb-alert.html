Curb Alert

Independent Proposal

The application's home screen features a social media-like feed that
captures users' attention by showing them local discoveries and posts in the area.



Upon tapping on a post from the main feed, users see an expanded image of the item, and are able to view and contribute to like/dislike feedback.
This feedback allows the application's administrators to keep track of, and potentially filter out, posts that are offensive or otherwise undesirable. Users can also watch and geolocate items, using the Locate button.






When users tap on the Locate button from the post detail screen, they can view a map with a pin that indicates the location of the item. They can also temporarily "claim" an item, and must pick it up within a given time frame, before it is re-released to the public.

The Challenge

Our team was tasked with proposing a mobile or desktop application that promoted environmental preservation and waste reduction. As I believe that excessive manufacturing and discarding of goods contributes to waste on a global scale, I thought that an application that encouraged the public to reuse unwanted items could be useful.

The Goal

We wanted to propose an easy-to-use application that, if developed, could encourage the public to recover discarded items from curbs and "free stuff" piles around their neighborhoods. I am a proponent of low waste lifestyles, and I believe that if users were exposed to a curated, social feed of useful items, they may be less inclined to spend money on items that could be acquired for free. Increased reusal of homewares and furniture like the ones that people discard every day could reduce the demand for excessive manufacturing and material waste.

Approach

Our team discussed requirements and key features for this proposed application, and mapped the information architecture. I functioned as the lead visual and interaction designer on the team.

The Outcome

We presented our proposal to a team of judges at Brooklyn Tech Week's Urban Hackathon.






The app's navigation expands from a hamburger menu on the left side of the display. Users can view and edit their profile, create posts, and access rewards for continued use of the app.
