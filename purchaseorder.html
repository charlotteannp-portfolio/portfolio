The Challenge

The client, a medical supply company, needed an internal tool to organize and track their purchase orders. This tool would be used primarily by project managers, and would contain information on products, partner companies, and contact information as well. The client had an existing tool to perform these tasks, but it was visually very complex and information-heavy, and there was no built-in method for filtering through order information.

The Goal

Reorganize information such that each screen contained only specific information, rather than as much information as possible on one screen. Clarify the application information architecture, such that users were able to orient themselves within the proper stage in the purchase order management process.

Approach

We met continually with the client team, and conducted the design process simultaneously with the development process. While the development team continued to refine the application functionality, I, as the lead designer, was in regular communication directly with the client team, who used the application themselves as part of their day-to-day responsibilities.

Because of our regular communication, I was able to get a thorough understanding of the requirements and scale of the application - for example, how many line items could be part of one order, and by which fields users were most likely to search when locating a specific line item. Periodically, throughout the design process, I sent iterations of my design work directly to the client team, who would review them based on their own needs, and send their feedback.

I also functioned as a front-end developer for this project, and I implemented the design updates into the final code base in HTML/CSS, with use of the Meteor framework.

The Outcome

The final version of this project became the client's primary tool for purchase order tracking.
